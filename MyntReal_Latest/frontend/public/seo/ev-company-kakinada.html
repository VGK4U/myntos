MyntReal — Kakinada's Electric Mobility Partner

From the deep-water port to the Kakinada SEZ, MyntReal is electrifying the commutes of East Godavari's most dynamic city.

Kakinada stands out in Andhra Pradesh's economic landscape as a city defined by its deep-water port, oil and gas industry concentration, and the presence of multinational energy companies. ONGC's Krishna Godavari Basin operations, GAIL's LNG terminal, HPCL's LNG import facility, and the Kakinada Special Economic Zone together employ a substantial white-collar and blue-collar workforce. This workforce commutes daily across Kakinada city — making it a uniquely concentrated EV buyer segment. MyntReal's Manthra EV electric two-wheelers address this demand with reliable LFP technology and a structured service network.

Beyond the industrial workforce, Kakinada has a significant fishing community, an active wholesale market, and an educational sector anchored by Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University (JNTUK). Students, shopkeepers, fishing harbour workers, and small business owners collectively form a large pool of daily commuters who would benefit from electric mobility's lower operating costs. MyntReal's EV dealership programme in Kakinada targets all these segments through an authorised retail and service network.

The Kakinada-Yanam Smart City initiative and the proposed Machilipatnam Port development further strengthen Kakinada's long-term growth prospects. MyntReal's EV franchise programme offers Kakinada entrepreneurs the ability to establish first-mover positions in a market poised for significant economic expansion. East Godavari district towns like Pithapuram, Tuni, Amalapuram, and Samalkota represent additional untapped EV territory with strong agricultural and commercial economies.

Kakinada's EV Market: Energy City's Clean Mobility Shift

It is particularly appropriate that Kakinada — one of India's oil and gas hubs — is now becoming a strong EV adoption market. The city's association with the energy sector has made its population analytically oriented toward energy costs: ONGC engineers and GAIL technicians who think about energy economics daily are well-placed to appreciate the cost advantages of electric mobility. Fleet operators servicing the SEZ's export logistics, fishery transport, and industrial commutes also benefit from EVs' lower per-kilometre operating costs compared to petrol alternatives. Kakinada's geographic position on the Bay of Bengal coast — with high solar irradiance — also makes it an ideal market for combined EV-and-solar products, giving MyntReal's multi-vertical franchisees natural cross-selling opportunities.
