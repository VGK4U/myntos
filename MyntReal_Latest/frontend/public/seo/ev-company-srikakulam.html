MyntReal — Srikakulam's EV Company of Choice

From Srikakulam town's commercial markets to the coastal fishing belts of Palasa and Narasannapeta, MyntReal is driving clean mobility across North AP.

Srikakulam district, stretching along the northeastern coast of Andhra Pradesh, is home to a large population of farmers, fishermen, weavers, and small traders whose daily mobility depends almost entirely on two-wheelers. The district headquarters — Srikakulam town — serves as the commercial and administrative hub, while coastal towns like Palasa-Kasibugga and Narasannapeta anchor the local economy. For this predominantly rural and semi-urban population, the shift from petrol to electric is no longer a futuristic aspiration — it is a financial necessity. MyntReal's Manthra EV two-wheelers offer exactly the right economics: low acquisition cost, near-zero fuel expense, and minimal maintenance.

Srikakulam's commuter distances — from residential colonies to markets, schools, fishing harbours, and government offices — are typically within 15–30 km per day, well within a single charge range of Manthra EV's LFP-battery platform. Local entrepreneurs and traders who take up EV dealership opportunities in Andhra Pradesh in Srikakulam are tapping a market where the practical case for EV adoption is already compelling, and the competitive landscape is still relatively open.

The Andhra Pradesh government's push for rural EV adoption and the National Electric Mobility Mission Plan have together created a policy environment that actively favours Srikakulam's transition to electric mobility. MyntReal's EV franchise programme in Andhra Pradesh provides local entrepreneurs with the brand, training, and supply chain support needed to build a sustainable EV business in Srikakulam — without the risk and uncertainty of a standalone startup.

Srikakulam's EV Market: Rural Mobility Going Electric

Srikakulam district's economy is anchored in agriculture, fishing, handloom weaving, and small-scale trade. With roughly 1.4 million people spread across 38 mandals and hundreds of villages, the district's mobility needs are predominantly short-range and high-frequency — a perfect match for affordable electric two-wheelers. The fishing community along the coast, the weavers of Narasannapeta, and the agricultural laborers of the interior mandals all share a common financial reality: petrol at Rs 100+ per litre is a significant monthly cost that an electric two-wheeler can slash by 80%. MyntReal's Manthra EV dealer network in Srikakulam is positioned to serve this transition by making EV acquisition accessible, reliable, and backed by local after-sales support.
