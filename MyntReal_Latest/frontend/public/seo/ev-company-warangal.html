MyntReal — Warangal's Electric Mobility Partner

From the ancient Kakatiya forts to NIT Warangal's modern campus, MyntReal's Manthra EV is charging the city's future.

Warangal is Telangana's second-largest city and one of South India's most historically significant urban centres. Known for the Ramappa Temple (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), the Warangal Fort, and the Thousand Pillars Temple, the city has deep cultural roots while simultaneously hosting some of Telangana's most important modern institutions. NIT Warangal — consistently ranked among India's top technical institutes — brings thousands of students and faculty to the city, while the KAKATIYA Urban Development Authority (KUDA) has overseen significant residential and commercial expansion. MyntReal's Manthra EV programme is positioned to serve Warangal's student, industrial, and commercial commuter base.

The JSW Steel plant at Kazipet, NTPC's projects in the region, and various textile manufacturing units provide Warangal with a substantial blue-collar industrial workforce that commutes daily. For these workers, electric two-wheelers represent a meaningful monthly saving on fuel — a factor that resonates strongly in Warangal's cost-conscious industrial community. MyntReal's EV programme in Telangana, adapted for Warangal, targets this workforce segment through authorised dealer points near industrial zones and residential colonies.

The Warangal-Hanamkonda-Kazipet triplet functions as a single urban agglomeration — distinct enough to support multiple EV dealer points but linked enough to share brand recognition. MyntReal's EV franchise partners in Telangana in this cluster benefit from network externalities: a customer who sees a Manthra EV showroom in Hanamkonda is more likely to buy from a Kazipet dealer, and vice versa. This cluster-city dynamic is one reason MyntReal is prioritising franchise territory allocation in Warangal early.

Warangal's EV Market: Heritage, Academia, and Industry

Warangal's economic structure combines academia, industry, and commerce in proportions that make it an exceptionally well-balanced EV market. The student population from NIT Warangal, Kakatiya University, and dozens of affiliated colleges creates a young, tech-savvy EV buyer cohort. Industrial workers from JSW Steel, BHEL's Warangal sub-station operations, and TSRTC bus depot commuters form a cost-sensitive but high-volume buyer group. And the city's commercial sector — textiles, handlooms, and the agricultural trade in surrounding mandals — generates a business owner buyer segment that values reliability and local service access. Manthra EV's combination of ISO-certified quality, EVolution-trained service network, and competitive pricing addresses all three segments simultaneously.
