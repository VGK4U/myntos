MyntReal — Guntur's Electric Mobility Partner

From Guntur's bustling chilli yards to Amaravati's emerging capital zone, MyntReal is building Guntur's clean transport future.

Guntur is one of Andhra Pradesh's most economically significant cities — often described as the chilli capital of India, hosting the country's largest chilli market alongside vast cotton and tobacco trading infrastructure. The city's large working population of traders, farmers, agricultural labourers, and market workers creates a high-volume commuter base where electric two-wheelers offer an immediate and measurable cost advantage. MyntReal's Manthra EV range, with its robust LFP battery and low maintenance design, is well-suited to Guntur's demanding mix of urban and semi-urban riding conditions.

Guntur's proximity to Amaravati — located approximately 20 kilometres away — positions it at the doorstep of AP's capital development zone. Infrastructure investment in the CRDA corridor has created a new class of upper-middle-income residents and business owners in Guntur who are receptive to premium EV products. MyntReal's EV dealership programme in AP offers Guntur entrepreneurs the opportunity to establish showroom businesses targeting both the traditional trading community and the newer capital corridor workforce.

The district hinterland — Tenali, Narasaraopet, Ponnur, Sattenapalli, and Piduguralla — represents a largely underserved EV market where MyntReal's EV franchise partners can build first-mover advantage. Agricultural communities in these towns travel long daily distances to Guntur markets on petrol two-wheelers; switching to electric would yield significant savings on a weekly basis. MyntReal's franchise model is deliberately designed to make this market transition commercially attractive for local entrepreneurs.

Guntur's EV Market: Agriculture Meets Urban Growth

Guntur district had one of the highest registered two-wheeler densities in Andhra Pradesh even before the current EV push. The city's economy — anchored in commodities trading, lace manufacturing, and agro-processing — means most business owners and workers are cost-sensitive buyers who evaluate every purchase on return-on-investment. Manthra EV's total cost of ownership pitch is particularly resonant in Guntur: lower fuel cost, low maintenance intervals, and government subsidy benefits translate to compelling economics for the city's practical-minded buyer base. The city also has an NRI community — primarily from the US and Australia — whose family members increasingly seek reliable, branded EV products rather than unbranded local alternatives. MyntReal's ISO 9001:2015 certification and structured business model address this quality-conscious buyer segment directly.

Why Guntur Is an Emerging EV Priority Market

Guntur's combination of a large, cost-conscious working population and its strategic position between Vijayawada and Amaravati makes it one of AP's fastest-emerging EV markets. The state government's plans for the Amaravati capital region are expected to increase Guntur's population and economic activity significantly. Land values in the Guntur–Tenali belt have already risen sharply, bringing with them a more affluent resident base. At the same time, the traditional agri-commercial economy continues to generate millions of vehicle trips daily. MyntReal's Manthra EV programme targets both segments simultaneously — the aspirational new-economy buyer and the cost-savings-focused traditional commuter. Franchise and dealership partners who enter Guntur now are building positions in a market that will be intensely competed in within three to five years.
