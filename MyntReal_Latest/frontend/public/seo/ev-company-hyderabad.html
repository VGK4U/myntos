MyntReal — Hyderabad's Electric Mobility Platform

From Gachibowli's tech campuses to Kompally's northern corridors, MyntReal's Manthra EV network is scaling across India's sixth-largest city.

Hyderabad is a city of superlatives — the sixth-largest city in India, the capital of Telangana, and the country's premier IT and pharmaceutical hub. With over 10 million residents spread across GHMC, HMDA, and the surrounding metropolitan area, Hyderabad is the single largest EV market in the region and one of the most important in South India. MyntReal's Manthra EV programme has established a presence in Hyderabad to capture the massive demand that the city's scale and demographics create for affordable, reliable electric two-wheelers.

The IT corridor stretching from HITEC City through Gachibowli, Kondapur, Madhapur, Nanakramguda, and Financial District employs well over half a million technology workers — a demographic that is educated, environmentally aware, and financially capable of making the switch to EVs. Traffic congestion on routes like ORR, NH 65, and the Jubilee Hills-Banjara Hills stretch has intensified interest in electric two-wheelers as the fastest and cheapest way to commute. MyntReal's EV dealer network in Telangana is expanding into all key Hyderabad corridors to meet this demand at scale.

Hyderabad's residential expansion into north Hyderabad (Kompally, Medchal, Shamirpet), east Hyderabad (Uppal, LB Nagar, Hayathnagar), and west Hyderabad (Miyapur, Bachupally, Chandanagar) has created new commuter towns that are underserved by public transport and where electric two-wheelers offer the most practical daily mobility solution. MyntReal's EV franchise programme in Telangana is structured to cover these emerging residential hubs with dedicated franchise territories.

Hyderabad's EV Market: Scale, Sophistication, and Speed

Hyderabad is not just Telangana's largest EV market — it is competing with Bengaluru and Pune as one of South India's top three EV adoption cities. The Telangana government's Electric Vehicle and Energy Storage Policy 2020-30 mandates EV-first procurement for government fleets and provides tax incentives for private EV buyers. The city's rapid metro rail expansion has already shifted some commuters, but the last-mile connectivity gap — from metro station to home or office — is largely served by electric two-wheelers. Ola Electric, Ather Energy, and TVS iQube have all made Hyderabad a priority market, validating the EV appetite here. MyntReal's Manthra EV enters this competitive landscape with the advantage of being the only EV brand with integrated solar, real estate, and insurance offerings — giving its Hyderabad dealers and franchisees multiple cross-selling revenue streams.

Why Hyderabad Demands a Serious EV Business Strategy

Hyderabad's EV market operates at a fundamentally different scale from any other city in the region. Its density, income diversity, geographic spread, and IT-sector concentration mean that EV franchisees and dealers need a different strategy than in tier-2 cities. MyntReal's Hyderabad playbook is built around zone exclusivity — preventing dealer conflict within the city — and corporate fleet programmes that access the IT sector's bulk purchasing capacity. The Telangana government's EV charging infrastructure roll-out, combined with the GHMC's electric bus replacement programme, is making EVs a mainstream conversation in Hyderabad households and offices. MyntReal's Hyderabad partners are building businesses at the right time — before the market reaches full competitive saturation, but with enough market awareness to convert rapidly.
