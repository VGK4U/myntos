MyntReal — Vijayawada's EV Company of Choice

From Auto Nagar's mechanical clusters to Benz Circle's commercial heart, MyntReal is electrifying Vijayawada's mobility landscape.

Vijayawada sits at the geographic and commercial centre of Andhra Pradesh. Often called the business capital of AP, the city processes a staggering volume of goods trade through its wholesale markets, Auto Nagar industrial cluster, and the sprawling CRDA region that bridges it to the planned capital Amaravati. With a registered two-wheeler population running into several lakhs, Vijayawada presents one of the most concentrated EV opportunity markets in the state. MyntReal's Manthra EV range addresses this market with affordable, reliable LFP-battery electric two-wheelers suited to Vijayawada's flat urban terrain and dense traffic patterns.

The cost calculus for Vijayawada commuters heavily favours switching to electric. Daily travel between residential colonies — Moghalrajpuram, Patamata, Benz Circle, Gunadala — and commercial zones in One Town or Autonagar costs petrol riders Rs 100–150 per day. A Manthra EV on a full charge costs a fraction, paying back its premium over a conventional two-wheeler within 12–18 months. MyntReal's EV dealership programme in AP is helping Vijayawada-based entrepreneurs capitalise on this demand shift by establishing authorised showrooms and service points.

The planned capital Amaravati, adjoining Vijayawada, is expected to drive significant population and infrastructure growth in the coming years. MyntReal's EV franchise partners in Andhra Pradesh who establish their presence in Vijayawada today position themselves for this upcoming growth. The city's excellent road connectivity via the NH 16 bypass and the Vijayawada–Hyderabad Expressway also makes it a natural logistics hub for expanding EV dealer operations into surrounding Krishna and Guntur district towns.

Vijayawada's EV Market: Trade Capital Meets Electric Future

Vijayawada is among AP's most densely populated cities, with an urban agglomeration exceeding 1.5 million people and a commercial culture that drives intense daily mobility. The city hosts Andhra Pradesh's largest wholesale markets for textiles, agricultural produce, and FMCG goods — generating enormous fleet and delivery vehicle movement. Business owners in these markets are acutely cost-conscious, making them natural early adopters of EVs when the economics are clearly communicated. MyntReal's Manthra EV fleet programme targets these segments with volume pricing and fleet maintenance contracts. Additionally, Vijayawada's student population — drawn by colleges in Governorpet, Moghalrajpuram, and Benz Circle — represents a strong two-wheeler EV buyer segment seeking economical daily transportation.
